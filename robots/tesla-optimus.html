Tesla Optimus

Humanoid
Tesla, Inc.
Humanoid Tesla AI Automation Bipedal

Tesla Optimus is a humanoid robot designed to perform manual tasks, blending AI with robotics for future labor automation.

Technical Specifications

Height 5 ft 8 in (1.73 meters)
Weight 125 pounds (57 kg)
Payload 20 pounds (9 kg)
Maximum Deadlift 150 pounds (68 kg)
Speed 5 miles per hour (8 km/h)
Actuators 28 structural actuators, 11 hand actuators
Processor Tesla's Full Self-Driving Computer (FSD Computer)
Power Battery pack (estimated 2.3 kWh)
Runtime Full day of work on a single charge (claimed)

Overview

Tesla's Optimus (also known as the Tesla Bot) is a general-purpose humanoid robot under development by Tesla, Inc. It was unveiled by Elon Musk during Tesla's AI Day event in August 2021. Designed to handle tasks that are repetitive, dangerous, or boring for humans, Optimus aims to revolutionize industries by introducing an affordable, autonomous, and safe labor force. Built with Tesla's expertise in AI, autopilot hardware, and energy systems, the robot represents Tesla's ambition to extend its AI capabilities beyond vehicles.

The robot features human-like hands with 5 fingers and precision grip capabilities. Its sensor suite includes autopilot cameras, FSD (Full Self-Driving) hardware, force feedback sensing, and tactile sensors, similar to those used in Tesla's autonomous vehicles.

Capabilities

Optimus is designed with several key capabilities:

  • Mobility: Walking at a moderate pace and navigating complex environments
  • Dexterity: Manipulating objects, using tools, and handling items of various weights
  • Perception: Understanding its environment via cameras and sensors
  • Cognition: Basic decision-making, simple conversations, and task learning through demonstrations
  • Safety: Features including lightweight materials and limited physical power to minimize risk to humans

Applications

Potential applications for Optimus span multiple sectors including:

  • Industrial: Manufacturing, warehouse logistics, material handling
  • Domestic: Household chores, running errands
  • Commercial: Retail, restaurants, and customer service roles
  • Healthcare: Supporting elderly or disabled individuals with daily activities
  • Space and Exploration: Assisting in extraterrestrial missions where human labor is costly and risky
